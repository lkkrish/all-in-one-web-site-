Tic-tac-toe

Tic-Tac-Toe, also known as Noughts and Crosses, is a classic two-player board game played on a 3x3 grid. The game’s objective is straightforward: each player takes turns marking a cell with their symbol (either “X” or “O”). The goal is to form a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of three of your symbols. The first player to achieve this wins the game. If all cells are filled and no player has won, the game ends in a tie

calculator

A calculator is a machine designed to automatically perform arithmetical operations and certain mathematical functions. Its origins trace back to the 17th century when Blaise Pascal devised a digital arithmetic machine in 1642, known as the Pascaline. Later inventors continued to refine these calculating machines, making them smaller and more user-friendly.
